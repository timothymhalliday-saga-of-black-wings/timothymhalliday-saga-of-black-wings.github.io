Before the Names Begin

The Living Heart of the Saga

The Saga of Black Wings is built from people who survive, choose, remember and refuse. Some arrive carrying blades. Others arrive carrying children, grief, secrets, medicine, food, feathers, scars or old songs. Some never speak with human tongues at all.

The Braid is not an institution, a bloodline or a command. It is a freely chosen bond between lives that continue to stand beside one another when standing becomes costly.

This hall is deliberately spoiler-safe. It reveals names, bonds and flavour without opening the saga’s locked doors too early.

The Living Braid

The Central Bonds

These relationships do not all bloom at once, nor do they follow one pattern. Each bond carries its own pace, wounds, humour, danger and language of care.

Raven-Hearted Warrior and Quiet Keeper

FATAS and Ness

FATAS is an oath-bearer, survivor and storm-centre whose greatest strength is his refusal to stop rising. Ness is the clear-eyed, steadfast force who helps keep the Braid from unravelling without claiming ownership of anyone’s road.

Endurance, honesty and the held Braid

Crown-Threaded Survivor and Scarred Protector

Cael and Brokan

Cael is clever, dangerous and difficult to read. Brokan is powerful, guarded and impossible to reduce to a simple judgement. Their road tests whether loyalty freely chosen can outlive fear and command.

Pressure, trust and chosen loyalty

Courage and Road-Loyal Companionship

Lucy and Jesse

Lucy carries warmth, memory and stubborn courage without allowing softness to become weakness. Jesse is grounded and brave, standing where friendship becomes action rather than promise.

Warmth, courage and action

Old Signs and Strong-Hearted Witness

Eldwyn and Ulrike

Eldwyn reads old knowledge while keeping mud on his boots. Ulrike brings grit, honesty and the strength to look directly at what others would rather leave unnamed.

Wisdom, grit and practical truth

Quiet Mystery and Future-Facing Courage

James and Maisie

James is thoughtful, wounded and road-marked, carrying hidden weight beneath a quiet manner. Maisie is a living thread of family, refusal and future law, never merely someone waiting to be protected.

Memory, courage and the unwritten future

The Wing-Pair

Scarlet and Blue

Scarlet, the female crow, watches with calm and garnet-shadowed judgement. Blue, the male raven, is inquisitive, iridescent and determined to notice every hidden door. Together they are witnesses, interrupters and a refrain running through the Braid.

Feather, warning and witness

Family Is a Living Verb

Hearth and Family

Parents of Joey and Lily

Sarah and Craig

Sarah’s care is practical, fierce and grounded in the daily work of keeping a family alive. Craig carries quiet strength, warmth and the kind of ordinary courage that holds its ground without spectacle.

Their children, Joey and Lily, carry curiosity, promise and the future-facing law of a family that refuses to count them small.

Sarah, Craig, Joey and Lily

Mother and Daughters

Josie, Katy and Jenny

Josie is a mother and protector whose endurance is woven through practical love. Katy and Jenny carry tenderness, courage and the ordinary human warmth that keeps epic stories from becoming hollow.

Care, protection and future promise

Bell-Light, Spirit Guide and Living Flame

Kian and Bella

Kian is a brave remembered name whose courage continues to ring through the saga. Bella is a presence of gentleness, memory and guidance, while Bella’s Flame burns as a living symbol of courage and refusal without becoming anyone’s possession.

Memory that still gives light

Grounded Companion

Taff

A thread of humour, loyalty and practical warmth within the wider world of the Braid. Taff helps keep great events tethered to the human scale of food, weather, friendship and getting on with it.

Humour, loyalty and hearth sense

Scent, Road and Hidden Truth

Those Who Read What Others Conceal

Scent-Bearer and Tracker

Jórunn

Sharp, instinctive and difficult to deceive. Jórunn reads scent, old-road guilt and truths people hoped their bodies would hide.

Watcher of Hidden Roads

Elland

Keeper of signs, secrets and old warnings. Elland knows too much, says too little and carries a history Jórunn may be able to read.

The Scent Woman

Vanessa

Connected to scent, memory and truths carried beneath ordinary notice. Her presence changes how the Braid reads the world around it.

“The body remembers roads the mouth has sworn never to name.”

The Forest Road

Kelda Veyrún

Druid and Keeper of Living Law

Kelda is a forest keeper of striking old-world beauty, rain-dark hair, green-brown eyes and quiet authority. Her strength is not ornamental. She listens before speaking, heals what can be healed, protects saplings, reads animal signs and cuts away rot when mercy can no longer be mistaken for delay.

The Great Bear

Kelda arrives with an enormous bear whose silence carries the weight of an older law. The animal appears to recognise Jack, Mylo, Scarlet and Blue, opening a road toward mysteries the Braid has not yet named.

Kelda and the bear belong to the bridge into Book XVII, Green Deer-Wood.

Those Who See

The Animal Witnesses

The animals of Black Wings are not mascots or ornaments. They warn, judge, remember, protect, interrupt and often understand the truth several chapters before the people do.

Female Crow Witness

Scarlet

Black-feathered with restrained burgundy and garnet. Scarlet is sturdy, calm, observant and frequently unimpressed by human folly.

Male Raven Witness

Blue

Slimmer, inquisitive and touched with cobalt-indigo iridescence. Blue notices unusual scents, hidden doors and trouble approaching.

Mixed-Breed Dog

Mad

Loyal, instinctive and gloriously unwilling to respect solemnity when solemnity has become ridiculous.

Brindle Dog

Benson

Grounded and dependable, carrying the strength of an animal who knows where home is and exactly who belongs there.

Black Dog

Hugo

Black-coated with white paws and pale ear tips. Hugo brings loyalty, warmth and a watchful spirit to the hearth.

Russet Dog

Shane

Red-coated, bright and full of road-energy. Shane carries the restless courage of a dog who would follow the Braid anywhere.

Sandy Siamese-Like Cat

Mylo

Clever, composed and magnificently unimpressed. Mylo watches larger animals with the confidence of someone certain the house is his.

Moor-Wolf

Jack

A wolf of shifting colour, old instinct and road-wisdom, standing at the border between beast, witness and legend.

Wing-Shadow Bird

Dusk

A dark, quiet bird-presence carried through shadow, warning and the changing hour between daylight and night.

Living Spirit Flame

Bella’s Flame

A sacred light of courage, memory and refusal, burning beside the Braid without ever becoming a possession.

The Living Rule

No One Is Owned Here

Names belong to the named. Care is not ransom. Love is not ownership. Blood is not command. Children are never proof. Refusal remains sacred. The Braid is offer, not net.
“They were not bound because they were trapped. They were bound because they stayed.”

The Other Side of the Hall

Not Every Name Comes in Peace

The saga also contains manipulators, traitors, collectors, false protectors and powers that believe love can be turned into ownership.
