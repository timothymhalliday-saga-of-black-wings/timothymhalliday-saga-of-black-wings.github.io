Before the Names Begin

The Saga of Black Wings is built from names. Some names are shouted across battlefields. Some are whispered under floors. Some are carried by ravens, wolves, dogs, children, ghosts, old roads and people who were told they did not matter.

This page is spoiler-safe. It introduces names, roles and flavour without opening the locked doors too early.

“A name remembered is a door the dark cannot fully close.”

The Braid

The Braid is not blood alone. It is chosen witness, dangerous love, shared road, stubborn humour, animal judgement, old oath and the refusal to let people be made small.

FATAS: raven-hearted warrior, oath-bearer, wound-carrier and storm-centre of the saga. Ness: beloved of the raven, fierce witness, healer of hard places and one of the saga’s deepest hearts. Mia: daughter, survivor, young woman of divided roads, grief, fire and dangerous becoming. Maisie: daughter, witness, fierce heart and central thread of love, refusal, family and future law. James: quiet mystery, wounded survivor, road-marked presence and one of the saga’s most important hidden hearts. Eldwyn: Hearth-bound guide, keeper of signs, old knowledge and practical stubbornness. Ulrike: strong-hearted presence of loyalty, grit, witness and sharp sense. Cael: Crown-threaded survivor, hidden operator and man caught between danger, loyalty and difficult truth. Brokan: scarred, powerful and impossible to file neatly under simple judgement. Jórunn Vánesja: scent-bearer, tracker, living witness of old Vánesja care and dangerous roads. Jesse: Braid presence of friendship, courage, witness and hard road loyalty. Lucy: Braid presence of love, witness and remembered courage, tied also to one of the saga’s most honoured names. Taff: a grounded, human thread in the wider Braid-world, carrying warmth, humour and ordinary loyalty.

The Wounded Road

Not every important person in Black Wings arrives clean. Some arrive damaged, pressured, misled, hunted, guilty, frightened or half-owned by terrible choices. The saga does not pretend wound and harm are simple things.

Bran: a wounded, troubled figure bound to one of the saga’s darker roads, carrying debt, pain and the possibility of change. Phoebe “Phebs”: a sharp, wounded presence whose path crosses guilt, survival, danger and difficult mercy. Myah: a deeply tangled figure tied to love, trust, family-law and one of the saga’s most painful emotional fractures. Riven: a marked presence of old hurt, road-shadow and the kind of survival that leaves teeth in the heart. Lark: a carried and protected name, bound to old danger, tenderness and the fierce law of not abandoning the vulnerable.

Familia and Hearth-Names

The saga’s wars are not only fought with blades. They are fought with food, warmth, stubborn care, laughter, shelter, witness and the refusal to let families be defined by the systems that wounded them.

Katy: a familia thread of love, memory, future and the child whose presence changes the shape of prophecy. Jenny: familia presence, carrying grounded love, human warmth and the ordinary strength that keeps people standing. Josie: familia presence, tied to care, belonging and the living world beyond battle. Little Hearth: child-thread, future-thread and small living proof that old systems do not get the final word. The Children of Witness Isle: small witnesses whose games, object-laws, fears and courage reshape the meaning of protection.

The Crowmere Thread

The Crowmere family belongs to the village-pressure, healer-wagon and old-remedy side of the saga: care, fear, guilt, habit, harm and the terrible question of when help becomes another kind of wrongness.

Krágr Halveth: a Crowmere healer tied to warm-handed care, old wrongness, family fear and the terrible question of when help becomes harm. Særa Veydís: a healer-mother figure caught between care, guilt, old remedies and the slow horror of comfort used wrongly. Lilja Crowmere: a Crowmere daughter with more truth in her than the systems around her expected, and the courage to speak late rather than never. Jórrik Salt-Marsh: a vulnerable Crowmere child-thread who hears roads too loudly and learns that hearing is not the same as obeying.

Beast and Bird Witnesses

The animals of Black Wings are not decoration. They judge, remember, warn, refuse, comfort, hunt, carry messages, expose lies and sometimes understand the law better than people do.

Mad: dog, guardian, patrol-heart and loyal beast with more sense than many official systems. Benson: beast-witness, companion, watcher and living argument against treating animals as property or tools. Hugo: beast-witness and loyal presence, often close to danger, scent, road and family. Blue: sharp-winged messenger, proud objector, loud opinion wrapped in feathers and one of the saga’s finest professional complainers. Scarlet: messenger-bird, sky-witness, route-reader and bearer of warning across dangerous roads. Dusk: quiet-winged witness, watcher of old routes and bird of shadowed intelligence. Jack: the Moor-Wolf, judge of hidden leashes, old animal law and the question of what is wild, trusted or falsely feared. Shane: dog-thread of pain, fear, loyalty and the complicated memory of what children survive. Mylo: salt-marsh cat, impossible courier, object-guardian and smug vanisher of great professional importance. Glimra: great Salt-Marsh Leopard, companion of old beast-druid power and northern danger.

Old Beasts and Fire-Wolves

Some beasts belong to the living road. Some belong to older ash, root-war, flame, hunger and stories told too late beside dying fires.

Cinder Wolves: fire-bound wolf-kind of ash, old roads, root-war and ancient fear. Where they run, root-magic remembers fire. The Cinder King: a vast fire-wolf presence of flame, ash and old beast-power. Not tame. Not safe. Not simple. The Valkryn Bone-Hound: old threat-shape of bone, pursuit and dread, remembered where bad roads grow teeth. Root-Hounds: root-made horrors of pursuit, obedience and old underground hunger.

Spirits, Watchers and Remembered Dead

In Black Wings, the dead do not always leave. Sometimes they guide. Sometimes they warn. Sometimes they stand quietly near the fire and make the living braver than they thought they could be.

Kian: brave little bell-light, spirit-warrior, remembered name and sacred presence whose courage echoes through the saga. Bella: spirit-guide, hearth-fire presence and loving witness whose gentleness has teeth against the dark. Elland: watcher, prophet, keeper of hidden signs and old warnings, with mud on his boots and secrets that refuse to stay buried. The Watcher Under the Floor: small witness of hidden childhood spaces, breath-counts, dust, fear and the law that the hidden child must be believed.

Hidden Kin and Northern Roads

As the saga moves north, family becomes more dangerous, not less. Hidden kin, mountain sickness, ransom logic and old agreements begin to test whether help can stay free when desperation bares its teeth.

Jórga Rósahaddr: rose-haired beast-druid of the Salt-Marsh, practical, sharp, half-feral and dangerous to underestimate. Marek Vey-Corren: hidden kin-thread tied to Elland, bloodline witness and the northern test of family without ownership. Hedda Vey-Corren: hidden kin-thread of the house beyond the house, bound to the dangerous question of what family can demand. Kelda Vey-Corren: hidden kin-thread, water-sign bearer and part of the northern road’s deepest family-law test. Finnr Vey-Corren: hidden child-thread of the Vey-Corren line, caught where ransom logic tries to turn need into ownership.

How to Read the Names

These entries are fragments, not verdicts. Some people change. Some fall. Some return. Some are loved before they are understood. Some are feared before anyone asks what taught the road to fear them.

“The Braid does not ask whether a name is tidy. It asks whether the living are still there.”
