The Hall of Names

The Saga of Black Wings is carried by names: some chosen, some stolen, some burned into law, some whispered by ravens, beasts, ghosts, and roads that refuse to forget.

This page introduces the major figures of the saga without spoiling the turns that make them dangerous. Some stand with the Braid. Some stalk it. Some are not people at all, but systems, hungers, laws, roads, roots, or old powers wearing the shape of a threat.

The Braid & Chosen Family

FATAS

Raven-marked warrior, witness, father, husband, refusal-bearer.

The central blade and battered heart of the saga. FATAS stands where blood, law, love, war, and memory collide, carrying old wounds without letting them become chains.

Ness

Hearth-warrior, chosen-life figure, love that refuses ownership.

Ness is warmth with iron beneath it: a woman whose love steadies the saga without softening its teeth. She stands beside FATAS not as shelter, but as a force the dark repeatedly fails to understand.

Mia

One of the divided daughters, gate-thread, survivor.

Mia carries a bloodline significance that others try to claim, read, use, or weaponise. Her story bends toward identity, agency, and the refusal to be reduced to a function.

Maisie

One of the divided daughters, judgement-bearer, living gate.

Maisie is sharp, wounded, brave, and not easily won back by apology. Around her, love must prove itself honest without demanding forgiveness as payment.

James

Quiet northern mystery, scar-bearer, Moor-Wolf-linked figure.

James listens where others speak. His silences carry tracks, scars, and old northern weather. He is central to threads that reach beyond easy blame and into older beast-law.

Eldwyn

Keeper of old craft, Hearth-Soothe bearer, long-arc witness.

Eldwyn carries skill, memory, and secrets from older roads. His path grows stranger as hidden marks and ancient warnings begin to point past the wars already known.

Ulrike

Reader of burned law, boundary-maker, truth-worker.

Ulrike sees what systems try to hide in ash, gaps, and official language. Her gift is not softness: it is precision, correction, and the refusal to let harm rename itself as order.

Brokan

War-brother, freed force, storm returning to the road.

Brokan’s presence changes the weight of any room. His return carries old violence, loyalty, grief, and the sense that some doors were never meant to hold him.

Cael

Undercover Crown-thread, risk-bearer, hidden ally.

Cael moves through danger by wearing masks near enemies who punish truth. His story belongs to infiltration, loyalty, and the cost of surviving among powers that make honesty lethal.

Jesse

Braid family, love-thread, grounded human courage.

Jesse brings warmth, humour, loyalty, and ordinary bravery into a world that keeps trying to make ordinary love impossible.

Lucy

Braid family, love-thread, witness of tenderness and fire.

Lucy’s place in the saga helps keep the human heart visible amid monsters, laws, roads, and war. She reminds the dark that joy is not weakness.

Katy

Living anchor, unborn-root thread, future-shaping mother.

Katy’s story touches prophecy, bloodline, and the terrifying question of what the old powers want from children not yet born.

Spirits, Guardians & Old Friends

Kian

Spirit-warrior, spear-mark, guardian presence.

Kian is not gone in the way the living understand gone. He stands in mist, violence, protection, and witness, a spirit whose silence can be sharper than a shouted oath.

Bella

Spirit-guide, hearth-flame, blue-white witness.

Bella carries warmth, warning, and judgement in a flame no ordinary dark can smother. She is guide, witness, guardian, and the quiet answer to many lies.

Elland

Keeper, watcher, old-road teacher, secret-bearer.

Elland moves through the saga like smoke with boots on. He knows more than he says, arrives late too often, and carries old work hidden under jokes, mud, and impossible timing.

Lark

Protected child-thread, old wound, living importance.

Lark’s name belongs to care, danger, and the fierce refusal to let the vulnerable become currency in another power’s design.

Animal Witnesses & Braid Beasts

Mad

Guardian dog, boundary-reader, practical judge.

Mad notices what polite people miss. If he refuses a step, a bowl, a room, or a silence, the wise start paying attention.

Benson

Braid dog, witness-body, refusal made fur.

Benson’s loyalty is not obedience. He helps show the saga’s living law: animals are witnesses, not tools.

Hugo

Braid dog, steady guardian, scent-and-silence witness.

Hugo stands beside danger with the honest gravity only a good dog can carry. His presence often means the room has already been judged.

Jack

Moor-Wolf, old beast-law, truth of the track.

Jack is not a pet and not an ornament. He belongs to older law, family-line mystery, northern roads, and the difference between fear and truth.

Blue

Raven witness, formal objector, sharp-tongued judge.

Blue sees too much and approves of very little. His objections are often funny, frequently inconvenient, and rarely wrong.

Scarlet

Messenger bird, scout, sky-thread of the Braid.

Scarlet carries warnings, routes, and witness across distance, helping the Braid stay connected when roads become dangerous.

Dusk

Messenger bird, scout, shadow-flight watcher.

Dusk belongs to movement, warning, and the old intelligence of wings in hostile weather.

Mylo

Salt-Marsh cat, Keeper-Courier, Elland-linked object guardian.

Mylo appears where cats should not, knows more than cats are willing to admit, and has a talent for finding hidden threads others miss.

Jórga Rósahaddr

Beast-Druid of the Salt-Marsh, animal-whisperer, old magic ally.

Known as Pink-Head or Rose-Haired, Jórga speaks beast-law more easily than people-law. She is strange, practical, sharp, and trusted by animals before humans earn the right.

Glimra

Mýrhlébarði, Salt-Marsh Sabre-Leopard, Veiltooth.

A massive camouflaging marsh apex-cat whose glimmering fur borrows the world around it. Glimra can stand close enough to breathe beside you and still remain unseen.

Witnesses, Law-Bearers & Children of Refusal

Jórunn Vánesja

Scent-worker, danger-reader, trace witness.

Jórunn reads ash, brine, wax, road marks, wool, tooth-ash, fear, and danger where others see nothing. Her nose is law’s enemy when law starts lying.

Edda

Returned child, no-bell law, refusal witness.

Edda carries a child’s law against spectacle, proof-display, and forced gratitude. Her presence teaches the Braid how rescue must not become display.

Evin

Returned child, road-dust witness.

Evin’s story belongs to roads, dust, and the way harm tries to file children as movement instead of people.

Lysa

Returned child, cracked cup, onion truth.

Lysa’s truths are small, stubborn, and devastating to false homes. She helps prove that ordinary details can break a lie’s grand mask.

Oris

Returned child, floor-lines, door-counting witness.

Oris counts what houses want hidden. His marks help turn fear into practical law.

Tovin

Returned child, bread-road law, oatcake clause.

Tovin helps reveal that roads can bite, hunger can be used, and even bread may carry a warning if someone knows how to witness it.

No Name Yet

Returned child, not-yet mark, one-two-one rhythm.

No Name Yet is not empty. Their refusal to be named too soon becomes a living lesson in consent, rhythm, and the space a person owns before the world demands an answer.

Villains, Threats & Broken Powers

The Crown

Systemic power, control structure, enemy of chosen witness.

The Crown is not only a throne. It is command, filing, ownership, correction, public order, and the belief that people become safer when made smaller.

Oath-Crown

Oath-bound authority, coercive legitimacy, old command logic.

Oath-Crown power dresses domination as duty. Its danger lies in making chains sound like vows.

The Duchess

Rootbound power, child-stealer, old enemy of the Braid.

The Duchess turns family, bloodline, and care into tools. Her cruelty rarely arrives bare-faced: it prefers ritual, claim, and the language of necessity.

The Rootbound

Root-claimed servants, living extension of old binding magic.

The Rootbound are what happens when life is bent into service beneath the soil. Their horror is not only what they do, but what was done to make them.

Súsanna Rótmóðir

Root-Mother, ancient arch-druid, originator of deeper root evil.

Older than the Duchess and more patient than ordinary monsters, Root-Mother treats bloodline, children, roots, and time as materials for a design that should never be allowed to finish.

The Underroot

Ancient root-network, living system, buried hunger.

The Underroot is not a simple place. It is memory, appetite, logic, root, and design beneath the visible world.

Eiðarr / Sawtooth

Poisoner of happiness, indirect antagonist, destabilising force.

Eiðarr ruins by pressure, whisper, arrangement, and rot. He does not always need to enter the room to poison it.

Blómblaða

Druid gone wrong, family-poisoner, enemy of FATAS and Ness.

Blómblaða carries hatred like a sacred duty. Where Eiðarr poisons happiness, she poisons family.

Myah

Infiltrator, traitor-thread, false-family danger.

Myah’s danger lies in being close enough to be trusted. Some betrayals do not begin with knives: they begin with being welcomed.

The Wrong Un’s

Counterfeit culture, distortion force, hostile imitation.

The Wrong Un’s are a threat that learns shape, language, habit, and comfort. Their horror is not only attack, but mimicry.

The Agreement

Ash-law system, false care, administrative threat.

The Agreement speaks in clean language while hiding cost, consent, and harm. It proves that paperwork can become a monster if no one is allowed to say no.

The Hollow

Emptying force, anti-self pressure, quiet hunger.

The Hollow threatens not only bodies but meaning. It works by making absence seem peaceful.

Alistair Venn

Agreement figure, procedural danger, clean-handed threat.

Alistair Venn belongs to the kind of threat that arrives with documents, authority, and the polished voice of reasonable harm.

Morcant Veyr

Power-broker, hidden pressure, old political danger.

Morcant Veyr’s name carries the smell of rooms where other people’s lives are arranged before they are consulted.

Grey Cradle / Thorn-Cradle Company

Child-route danger, commercialised harm, clean verb thief.

Grey Cradle and Thorn-Cradle turn children into routes, records, transfers, and arrangements. Their evil hides in tidy words.

Article Ash-Seven

Burned law instrument, custody threat, missing safeguard.

Article Ash-Seven is a piece of law that should have protected people, but arrives burned, altered, and hungry for interpretation.

Skeld-Fell Ransom Faction

Northern threat, ransom power, cold-road pressure.

The Skeld-Fell ransom faction belongs to the northern arc: cold bargains, family pressure, mountain danger, and the price enemies place on blood.

The True Cold Beneath Skeld-Fell

Ancient cold power, old bargain, hidden northern threat.

Beneath Skeld-Fell waits something older than immediate villains. It is not merely weather. It is bargain, memory, and cold with intention.

Cold Hearth Hall

Northern stronghold, family-harm seat, cold authority.

Cold Hearth Hall is less a home than a pressure system: a place where warmth has rules, fire has teeth, and welcome may carry a price.

Ransom House of Snow

Skeld-Fell-linked threat, hostage logic, cold family weapon.

The Ransom House of Snow turns kinship, debt, winter, and fear into leverage.

Other Named Powers & War-Forces

Valkryn Bone-Hound

Hound-threat, old war-beast, bone-law terror.

A name that belongs to pursuit, fear, and the kind of beast-power that makes roads feel hunted.

Hraeldir the Oath-Breaker

Broken-oath figure, old treachery, saga-shadow.

Hraeldir’s name carries the stink of vows shattered for power. Whether legend, man, or warning, the title alone is enough to poison a hall.

The Cinder Host

Burned war-force, ash-march, hostile host.

The Cinder Host brings the sense of war after flame: not bright battle, but what keeps walking when the burning should be over.

Torvik Brandhand

Fire-marked enemy, branded force, war-name.

Torvik Brandhand is a name fit for scorched doors, burned claims, and the handprint violence leaves when it wants to be remembered.

The House

Memory-place, childhood wound, threshold threat.

The House is not just walls. It is memory, pressure, old fear, silence, inheritance, and the question of whether a place that raised someone ever had the right to keep them.
