Before the Names Begin

The Living Heart of the Saga

The Saga of Black Wings is built from people who survive, choose, remember and refuse.

Some arrive carrying blades. Some arrive carrying children, grief, secrets, medicine, food, feathers, scars or old songs. Some never speak with human tongues at all.

This page is spoiler-safe. It offers names, bonds and flavour without opening the saga’s locked doors too early.

The Living Braid

The Central Bonds

The Braid is not an institution, bloodline or command. It is a freely chosen bond between lives that continue to stand beside one another when standing becomes costly.

Raven-Hearted Warrior

FATAS

Oath-bearer, survivor, wound-carrier and storm-centre of the saga. His greatest strength is not conquest, but the refusal to stop rising.

Beloved and Fellow Traveller

Ness

Fierce, loving and clear-eyed. Ness does not arrive as reward or rescue, but as a woman willing to walk beside a wounded warrior without owning his road.

Crown-Threaded Survivor

Cael

Clever, dangerous and difficult to read. Cael survives among hidden pressures while trying to remain loyal to truths stronger than command.

Scarred Protector

Brokan

Powerful, guarded and impossible to reduce to a simple judgement. Beneath the scars stands a man learning what freely chosen loyalty can become.

Witness of Courage

Lucy

A presence of love, memory and stubborn courage. Lucy carries warmth without allowing softness to become weakness.

Road-Loyal Companion

Jesse

Brave, grounded and loyal through difficult roads. Jesse stands where friendship becomes action rather than promise.

Keeper of Signs

Eldwyn

Hearth-bound guide, reader of old knowledge and keeper of practical truths. Eldwyn brings wisdom with mud still on its boots.

Strong-Hearted Witness

Ulrike

Loyal, sharp-minded and difficult to shake. Ulrike brings grit, honesty and strength to the places where others might look away.

Quiet Mystery

James

Wounded, thoughtful and road-marked. James carries hidden weight and one of the saga’s most quietly important hearts.

Fierce Heart of the Future

Maisie

Daughter, witness and living thread of family, refusal and future law. Maisie is never merely someone waiting to be protected.

Family Is a Living Verb

Hearth and Family

Mother and Hearth-Witness

Sarah

A mother whose care is practical, fierce and grounded in the daily work of keeping a family alive.

Father and Steady Hand

Craig

A family man of quiet strength, warmth and ordinary courage. Craig stands as proof that gentleness can hold its ground.

Daughter of the Hearth

Lily

Bright, observant and woven into the future of the living Braid. Her courage grows in the company of those who refuse to count her small.

Son of the Hearth

Joey

Child of Sarah and Craig, carrying curiosity, courage and the future-facing law of the family.

Mother and Protector

Josie

Mother of Katy and Jenny, tied to love, care, endurance and the grounded strength of family.

Daughter and Future-Thread

Katy

A young life of courage, tenderness and future promise, whose presence changes the shape of what comes next.

Daughter and Hearth-Light

Jenny

Warm, human and deeply woven into the ordinary love that keeps great stories from becoming hollow.

Grounded Companion

Taff

A thread of humour, loyalty and practical warmth within the wider world of the Braid.

Fire, Spirit and Memory

Kian and Bella

Bell-Light and Spirit Warrior

Kian

A brave remembered name whose courage continues to echo through the saga. Small in years, never small in meaning.

Spirit Guide

Bella

A loving presence of gentleness, memory and guidance. Bella’s light is warm, but never defenceless.

Living Spirit Flame

Bella’s Flame

A sacred flame of courage, memory and refusal. It burns beside the Braid without becoming anyone’s possession.

Scent, Road and Hidden Truth

Those Who Read What Others Conceal

Scent-Bearer and Tracker

Jórunn

Sharp, instinctive and difficult to deceive. Jórunn reads scent, old-road guilt and truths people hoped their bodies would hide.

Watcher of Hidden Roads

Elland

Keeper of signs, secrets and old warnings. Elland knows too much, says too little and carries a history Jórunn may be able to smell.

The Scent Woman

Vanessa

A woman connected to scent, memory and truths carried beneath ordinary notice. Her presence changes how the Braid reads the world around it.

Green Deer-Wood

The Forest Keeper

Druid and Keeper of Living Law

Kelda Veyrún

A forest keeper of striking old-world beauty, rain-dark hair, green-brown eyes and hidden authority.

Kelda listens before speaking, heals what can be healed, cuts away rot and refuses to mistake mercy for weakness.

Witness-Bear

The Great Bear

A vast forest companion who arrives beside Kelda and appears to recognise Jack, Mylo, Scarlet and Blue.

The bear carries the quiet weight of an older law.

Black Wings Above

The Bird Witnesses

Scarlet and Blue are not decoration, mascots or heraldic furniture. They judge, warn, interrupt and remember.

Female Crow Witness

Scarlet

Black-feathered with restrained burgundy and garnet. Scarlet is sturdy, calm, observant and frequently unimpressed by human foolishness.

Male Raven Witness

Blue

Slimmer, inquisitive and touched with cobalt-indigo iridescence. Blue notices hidden doors, unusual scents and trouble before anyone invites it inside.

Wing-Shadow Bird

Dusk

A dark, quiet bird-presence carried through shadow, warning and the moments between daylight and night.

The Hearth Pack

Dogs, Wolves and the Cat Who Judges Them

Mixed-Breed Dog

Mad

Loyal, instinctive and gloriously unwilling to respect solemnity when solemnity has become ridiculous.

Brindle Dog

Benson

A grounded, dependable witness whose presence carries the strength of an animal who knows where home is.

Black Dog

Hugo

Black-coated with white paws and pale ear tips. Hugo brings loyalty, warmth and a watchful spirit to the hearth.

Russet Dog

Shane

Red-coated, bright and full of road-energy. Shane carries the restless courage of a dog who would follow the Braid anywhere.

Sandy Siamese-Like Cat

Mylo

Clever, composed and magnificently unimpressed. Mylo observes the larger animals with the confidence of someone certain the house belongs to him.

Moor-Wolf

Jack

A wolf of shifting colour, old instinct and road-wisdom. Jack belongs to the borderlands between beast, witness and legend.

Keeper of Courage

The Living Rule

No One Is Owned Here

Names belong to the named. Care is not ransom. Love is not ownership. Blood is not command. Children are never proof. Refusal remains sacred. The Braid is offer, not net.
“They were not bound because they were trapped. They were bound because they stayed.”

The Other Side of the Hall

Not Every Name Comes in Peace

The saga also contains manipulators, traitors, collectors, false protectors and powers that believe love can be turned into ownership.
