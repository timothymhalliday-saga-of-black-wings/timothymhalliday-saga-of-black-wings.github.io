The Road So Far

The Saga of Black Wings begins with ash, hunger, violence and flight, then widens into a vast mythic war against crowns, roots, agreements, false mercy, inherited wounds and powers that try to make people smaller than they are.

This page gives a spoiler-safe guide to each book. It is built for new readers, curious wanderers, future collectors and anyone who hears ravens in the weather.

“They were wounded. They were weary. They were many. They were faithful.”

Finished Manuscripts

Book I
Saga of Black Wings

The first road begins in ash, pursuit and survival. FATAS, Ness and the early Braid are shaped by hunger, violence, children in danger, old enemies and the first hard truth of the saga: no one survives alone.

First Road FATAS & Ness Cinder Threats

Book II
The Reckoner’s Knot

The road tightens into darker reckoning. Old choices begin tying themselves into knots, and the Braid learns that survival is never clean when power keeps ledgers of pain.

Reckoning Oaths Hard Roads

Book III
The Crown of Thorns and Salt

Crowns, salt, thorns and old claims rise into sharper focus. The saga’s world deepens, and the Braid faces the cost of standing against powers that believe people can be owned.

Crown Law Salt-Marsh Old Claims

Book IV
The Crown Unmade

Authority cracks. The Crown’s shape begins to fail, but unmaking a crown does not unmake everything it taught the world to fear, obey and repeat.

The Crown Severance Aftermath

Book V
The War of the Underroot

Beneath broken authority, older hunger wakes. The Underroot rises through memory, blood, soil and old design, forcing the Braid to fight what lives below foundations.

Underroot Root-Horror Bella’s Flame

Book VI
The Rising Root

The root-war deepens. The Braid confronts the terrible patience of buried systems, where corruption does not simply attack, but waits until people mistake it for nature.

Root-Mother Buried Law Old Magic

Book VII
The Severing War

Severance becomes more than cutting. Love, war, prophecy, children, animals and old signs collide as the Braid learns that to sever cleanly, one must first know what must never be cut away.

Severing Romance Rune-Layers

Book VIII
The Last Root

The root-war reaches toward its deepest shape. The Braid faces the last root not as a simple monster, but as a final hunger for meaning, completion and control.

Last Root Final Hunger Choice

Book IX
The War Without Root

After the root breaks, the world does not become safe. It becomes unfinished. Absence, agreement, memory and identity begin to shift, and the Braid faces a war where reality itself starts asking what is worth keeping.

Absence Agreement Identity

Book X
The Quieting World

The world learns softer ways to erase. Quiet becomes a weapon, peace becomes suspicious, and the Braid must answer powers that do not always conquer by shouting.

The Quiet False Peace Witness Isle

Book XI
The Agreement of Ash

False mercy rises in ash-coloured clothing. The Agreement offers relief, shelter and order, but its kindness carries hooks. The Braid must learn how to refuse help that costs too much.

Agreement Ash Law Child-First

Book XII
The Last Refusal

Refusal becomes sacred ground. The saga turns toward consent, care, distance, danger and the hard truth that protection must never become ownership.

Refusal Consent Care Without Claim

Book XIII
The House That Raised the Raven

The war turns inward. FATAS faces the House that shaped him, Ness stands beside him, and the saga asks whether a person can be shaped by violence without belonging to it.

The House FATAS & Ness Chosen Home

Book XIV: In Development

Book XIV
The House Beyond the House

Book XIV has not started yet. The planned road moves north toward hidden family, Skeld-Fell, cold hearths, ransom, beast-law and the dangerous question of whether desperation can ever become permission.

Its guiding truths are already burning: desperation is not permission, and bloodline is witness, not ownership.
