Timothy M. Halliday

Writing as T.M.H, Timothy M. Halliday is the creator of The Saga of Black Wings, an epic fantasy world more than forty years in the making.

Forty Years in the Making

Timothy M. Halliday has carried and shaped The Saga of Black Wings for around forty years. This is not a world made overnight, nor a story built quickly from spare parts. It is a lifetime of imagination, emotion, memory, myth, humour, grief, loyalty, animals, wounds, roads, houses, ravens, wolves, roots, crowns, and stubborn hope.

What began as private imagination grew over decades into a vast fantasy saga: a living world of characters, odes, songs, battles, laws, beasts, hidden families, old magic, dangerous roads, and places that remember more than they should.

Forty years in the making. One saga still unfolding.

The books carry the weight of a world that has been lived with for a long time. The small details matter. The names matter. The animals matter. The songs, jokes, wounds, houses, doors, and silences all have roots beneath them.

The Heart of the Saga

At its heart, The Saga of Black Wings is about people and creatures who refuse to be owned by the powers that shaped them. It is about found family, old wounds, dangerous inheritance, fierce loyalty, strange magic, loyal animals, and the difficult work of surviving without becoming what hurt you.

The saga blends epic fantasy, folklore, emotional realism, mythic violence, humour, poetry, odes, runes, spirit, beasts, and ancient law. It moves through crowns, roots, roads, houses, ash, refusal, love, betrayal, and the deep living power of place.

The world of Black Wings is dark at times, but it is not built only from darkness. It is also built from laughter, friendship, animal humour, stubborn tenderness, beautiful landscapes, impossible courage, and the warmth of those who choose to stand beside one another when the world becomes cruel.

A Personal Epic

Timothy’s fiction is deeply personal. The saga carries echoes of life itself: good and bad times, grief and joy, pain and laughter, past and present animals, friends, neighbours, strangers, places, and moments that leave their mark.

Across the books, those echoes become marshes, roads, houses, ravens, wolves, hounds, leopards, roots, fires, oaths, and bloodlines. Real feeling becomes invented legend. Private endurance becomes myth. A lifetime of thought and imagination becomes a black-winged road.

After writing in secret for much of his life, Timothy is now preparing The Saga of Black Wings for readers beyond the locked room. The result is both invented fantasy and personal testament: a long, strange, emotional, funny, fierce, and unforgettable saga that refused to die.
