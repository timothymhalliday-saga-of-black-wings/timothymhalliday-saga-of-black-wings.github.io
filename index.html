The Braid, the Beasts, and the Broken Powers

The Saga of Black Wings is not built on one hero alone. It is a living braid of lovers, family, witnesses, animals, spirits, songs, scars, old laws and enemies who should have stayed buried. Some names below are central from the beginning. Some wait in the dark. Some are best met carefully.

“No road is walked alone.”

The Braid & Chosen Family

FATAS

The Ferocious, Raven of the Black Wings.

Warrior, survivor, father, witness and storm-bearer. FATAS carries love like a weapon that refuses to become cruelty, and grief like a road that still must be walked.

Ness

Crow of the hearth, keeper of rooms, love with teeth.

Ness is not the quiet heart because she is soft. She is the hearth because she endures heat, feeds the living, and knows exactly when tenderness must draw a blade.

Eldwyn

Hearth-bound druid, reader of old law and broken signs.

Eldwyn hears what the land says beneath ordinary sound. He carries old craft, old regret, and the dangerous wisdom of knowing that signs always demand a cost.

Ulrike Ellandsdóttir Róthvaldr

Raven-root scholar, burned-law reader, inheritance refuser.

Ulrike enters the saga like an old prophecy with boots on. She reads what others cannot, but refuses to let lineage decide what she must become.

Mia

Daughter, deep-root feeling, one half of the divided gate.

Mia carries structure as strength rather than surrender. Her heart becomes one of the saga’s most delicate battlefields, especially where love and trust are tested.

Myah

Druid-born, close to Mia, bound to a deep family wound.

Myah is fierce, beautiful, dangerous to simplify, and tied to one of the saga’s sharpest emotional threads. Her closeness to Mia makes her story impossible to treat lightly.

Maisie

Wild-winged daughter, watcher-touched and wind-minded.

Maisie carries disruption like a sacred refusal. Where old systems seek obedience, she brings interruption, sharp sight and a grin that refuses to kneel.

James

Quiet mystery, northern echo, beast-bound thread.

James is one of the saga’s deep waters: still on the surface, old signs beneath, and watched by creatures who know more than people say aloud.

Lucy

Grief-bearer, blade-heart, living continuation of loss.

Lucy carries grief like an unsheathed thing and still does not turn from the road. Her courage is not loud. It is staying when grief wants the world emptied.

Jesse

Road-wise survivor, loyal shadow, hard-won tenderness.

Jesse knows routes, risks and old shadows. Beside Lucy, he proves that devotion can survive battle without becoming fragile.

Cael

Hidden inside power, patient as a sheathed blade.

Cael moves through machinery, secrets and difficult truth. He knows how to survive among wolves without becoming one.

Brokan

Chainbreaker, grief-worn, unbowed.

Brokan is built from endurance, work and held silence. His road bends through loss, but not surrender.

Lúsaith / Lark

Marked child, bright Lark, tenderness sharpened by survival.

Lúsaith chooses the smaller name Lark. Her wrist carries an old claim, but she is not the claim. She remains a child before any power can turn her into a key.

Timóði

Forgotten prince, title-wounded, becoming more than power made him.

Timóði walks out from under old names and ruined title. To guard is not enough for him. He must remember how to belong.

Taff of Winchmore / Salt-Raven

Silent leader, hand-speaker, salt-marsh watcher.

Taff proves that speech does not belong only to sound. His signs carry warning, secrecy, endurance and lightning enough for any room wise enough to watch.

Jórunn Vánesja

Scent-woman, trace-bearer, old knowing in human form.

Jórunn reads what the world leaves behind: fear, blood, milk, lies, ash, old roads and hidden hands. If the air has been touched by danger, she may know before the knives do.

Katy

Mother, refusal-bearer, protector of the child first.

Katy stands against every power that tries to turn a child into a key, prophecy, weapon, debt or door. Her law is simple and absolute: child first.

Family, Children & Witnessed Lives

Little Hearth

Katy’s child, future held as child before prophecy.

Little Hearth is not a key, claim, door or weapon. The saga insists on this before any old law can open its hungry mouth.

Jenny

Raven-Crow bloodline, watcher-thread.

Jenny carries a quiet old sensitivity, the sort of instinct that notices pressure before pressure becomes a hand.

Josie

Raven lineage, marsh-root memory.

Josie belongs to the family lines where old memory does not always sleep cleanly. Through her blood and daughters, buried magic finds new doors.

Craig

Book XII family group, witness-thread.

Craig belongs to the family roads around the Last Refusal, where ordinary names are pulled into extraordinary witness.

Sarah

Book XII family group, care and witness.

Sarah’s place in the wider family thread helps keep the saga grounded in living households, not just battlefields and old powers.

Lily

Book XII family group, child-law thread.

Lily belongs among the names protected by the saga’s hardest law: children are not proof, payment, prophecy or debt.

Joey

Book XII family group, protected name.

Joey stands within the family web where the Braid’s refusal becomes personal, practical and fiercely human.

Riven

Questioner, witness, child-law voice.

Riven asks the sort of questions that cut cleaner than blades. In a world of ledgers and old powers, a true question can become a shield.

Edda Fen-Tarn

Outer-door elder, careful child-witness.

Edda knows that no rescued child should be rung like a victory bell. Her refusals help teach Witness Isle how to care without claiming.

Evin

Child of refusal, protected voice.

Evin belongs to the saga’s child-law heart: no voice is too small to matter when a power tries to make silence useful.

Lysa

Child of refusal, living answer.

Lysa stands against every system that wants a child to become a neat line in someone else’s ledger.

Oris

Child of refusal, name held safely.

Oris reminds the saga that rescue must not become ownership, and that protection must still leave room for choice.

Tovin

Child of refusal, road-held witness.

Tovin’s place belongs to the hard kindness of waiting, listening and refusing to make a child pay rent for being saved.

No Name Yet

Unclaimed child, name protected by waiting.

In this saga, not naming can be a form of protection. Later is a real place, and waiting may be the truest shelter.

Tomas

Brackenmere child, keeper of Bravefen.

Tomas reminds the Agreement that a child’s toy can carry truth heavier than a courtroom. Some little horses return with thunder in their broken legs.

Bram

Child-witness of the ash years.

Bram belongs among the small voices who prove that children hear the hidden meaning in rooms before adults admit there is one.

Old Vellum

Keeper of old record and shaken testimony.

Old Vellum carries the ache of records that should have protected the living, and too often became shelves for harm instead.

Nema

Truth-speaker of buried horror.

Nema names what others tried to soften. In a saga where false mercy thrives on quiet, plain horror spoken honestly becomes a lantern.

Animal Witnesses & Beast-Kin

Mad

Guardian dog, danger-sense, beloved chaos with teeth.

Mad is not decoration. He is warning, warmth, comic thunder and living witness. When Mad refuses a place, wise people stop walking.

Benson

Hound of loyalty, watch and road-memory.

Benson belongs to the older animal heart of the saga: trained, tested, loyal and able to feel truths humans keep trying to talk around.

Hugo

Hound, companion, four-footed witness.

Hugo helps carry the animal soul of the Braid: practical, watchful and wonderfully unwilling to respect human nonsense.

Jack

Moor-Wolf, Mýrulfr, blue-white northern mystery.

Jack is not a pet. He is an ancient intelligent Moor-Wolf, tied to old northern signs, James, beast-law and the wild witness of Lake Vaelthorn country.

Blue

Crow, scout, comic blade in feathered form.

Blue watches, comments, steals dignity from solemn moments and often knows too much before anyone else admits there is something to know.

Scarlet

Raven, messenger, red-winged warning.

Scarlet carries messages, omens and sharp feathered presence through the saga’s war-roads. A raven is never just scenery here.

Dusk

Scout-bird, shadow-wing, sky witness.

Dusk belongs to the air above the Braid, where warning can arrive on wings before the road itself admits danger.

Mylo

Animal witness, loyal presence.

Mylo adds to the living company of beasts who know the difference between fear, threat, grief and lies before people can put words to them.

Shane

Red northern Salt-Hound, old joy from FATAS’s childhood.

Shane hates closed doors, steals bread with unlawful joy, and carries one of the few bright things to survive the House that raised the Raven.

Mother Hiss

Goose-witness, household terror, comic law on webbed feet.

Mother Hiss proves that not every guardian needs fangs. Some arrive honking, offended, and certain the whole world requires correction.

Spirits, Dead Guardians & Old Watchers

Kian

Marsh-Heralded, spear in the mist, witness beyond death.

Kian does not simply vanish when death takes him. He becomes mist, warning, oath-weather and the witness who keeps standing where love refuses to break.

Bella

Blue-white flame, spirit-watch, hearth beyond breath.

Bella burns with a flame that judges warmth, return and truth. She is comfort, but never comfort made stupid.

Elland

Old druid, watcher beneath the shaping of the world.

Elland remains in reeds, signs, fragments and warnings. What was lost was never fully lost. What was buried remained speaking.

Jorn

Companion of the unseen, listener to the dead.

Jorn walks near thresholds where the dead are not always silent. His place in the saga is quiet, loyal and strange in all the right places.

Sampher

Ancient presence, beast-speaker, one the marsh remembers.

Sampher enters with a peace too old to trust blindly and too real to dismiss. Even the animals know him before explanation can catch up.

Williem

Child-shaped mystery, old joy through beast and bird.

Williem speaks where dogs and birds understand without translation. He reminds the Braid that some ancient things arrive wearing small faces.

The Seer

Blind watcher of first roads and old debts.

The Seer arrives where prophecy smells of ash and consequence. Her truths rarely comfort, which is often how the living know they matter.

Road, Marsh & Witness Isle Names

Rúnhald Skald-Tongue

Reed-Singer of the Salt-Marshes, keeper of saga-song.

Rúnhald gives the age a voice when memory needs more than record. In his odes, grief becomes a shape the future can carry.

Runa

Early-road protector, keeper of small lives.

Runa belongs to the first road, where smoke, hunger and frightened children shaped the first Braid before anyone had a grand name for it.

Wren

Road-room name, brine-shadow witness.

Wren’s name belongs to the early web of rooms, warning, poor choices and the price of selling a key too cheaply.

Rook

Hard road presence, watcher of consequences.

Rook stands among the rough-edged names who remind the saga that survival is rarely tidy, and mercy still needs a door kept shut.

Sella

Questioning voice from the first road.

Sella brings the necessary discomfort of asking when punishment becomes too much, and why safety must sometimes be guarded without pleasure.

Old Mera

Settlement elder, survivor of ash and road.

Old Mera belongs to the ordinary lives that make the saga larger than its battles. Elders, water skins, burned roofs and stubborn survival all matter here.

Ragna

Brinestone hand, raid-weather presence.

Ragna brings rough strength, old road humour and the kind of presence that turns an ambush into a story told with teeth.

Styrbjörn / Stuart / Battle-Bear

Crossing-holder, stubbornness made architecture.

Styrbjörn stands where crossings need more than courage. He is a gate that decided to be a mountain.

Edwill

Returned road-name, old difficult truth.

Edwill reminds the Braid that the past does not stay buried just because the living prefer it quiet.

Phoebe

Marked by coin-contract, pain-bound but not ended.

Phoebe carries the possibility that what has been used might still refuse its user. Not every redemption is bright. Some arrive scarred and trembling.

Bran

Pain-bound, hunted by choices not wholly his.

Bran’s road bends toward hard consequences and incomplete redemption. The Braid makes room for fractured returns as well as clean victories.

Karla

Witness of the War Without Root.

Karla sees the world begin refusing to confirm what it once held. Her reports help name the horror before the horror becomes ordinary.

Thom

Uncertain witness, almost unmade by agreement.

Thom becomes one of the saga’s strangest warnings: a person can remain visible while the world begins negotiating whether he fully belongs.

Edrik Vale

Witness to absence and the failure of certainty.

Edrik Vale’s words preserve one of the first truths of the War Without Root: if something can erase a place, it can learn to erase a person.

Renn, Hale & Mera

Names called back from uncertainty.

These names belong to the frightening edge of Book IX, where calling someone may bring back an echo rather than the person you meant.

Dalla Wren

Road-witness, small-bird sharpness.

Dalla Wren carries the sort of name that sounds light until the story reveals what small wings have survived.

Perren

Witness-thread, road-name.

Perren belongs to the widening company of names whose presence helps the world feel older, fuller and watched from more than one doorway.

Tavren Cole

Road-echo, ember-name, dangerous witness.

Tavren Cole carries a name with ash and road in it, a figure to be met where the saga’s human texture thickens and darkens.

Rogues, Tyrants & Human Threats

Doc

Clean coin, trained hounds, calm cruelty.

Doc waits with patience, rooms that obey him too well, and the kind of quiet that makes a whistle more frightening than a shout.

Hrothar Vael

The Cinder-King.

Hrothar Vael does not need to run when others can be made to run toward him. His hunger waits behind the smoke roads.

Skorn Grey-Tooth

Ash-reader, Cinder-road threat.

Skorn belongs to the first road’s hunting pressure, where fear becomes trail and ash becomes instruction.

Torvik Brandhand

Flame-carrier, burned hand, named threat.

Torvik Brandhand sounds like a man who has made pain into a signature. That sort of name should never be met casually.

Maera Salt-Eye

Sea-measurer, road-pressure, early threat.

Maera Salt-Eye belongs to the hunted coast and first-road pressure, where watching can become its own kind of blade.

The Duchess

Rootbound power, polished command, cultivated threat.

The Duchess belongs to the old machinery of control: refined, dangerous and skilled at making cruelty sound like necessity.

Eiðarr / Sawtooth

Poisoner of happiness, private hatred given teeth.

Eiðarr ruins joy because joy proves people are not his. He is a wound with a grin and an appetite for spoiling what others love.

Blómblaða of Vardrum

Petal, druid gone wrong, poisoner of family.

Blómblaða hates the Braid with a clean, venomous devotion. Where Eiðarr poisons happiness, she poisons family.

Alistair Venn

Reasonable man beside the bowl.

Alistair Venn is dangerous because he does not need to shout. He arranges surrender until it looks like courtesy.

Morcant Veyr

Thorn-Cradle rot, old druid harm, child-road shadow.

Morcant Veyr carries the smell of old cages, sleep-heavy milk and roads where children were treated as things to be kept before they became choices.

Systems, Old Powers & Places That Behave Like Villains

The Crown

Power, command and ownership dressed as order.

The Crown is not merely a throne. It is a system that teaches people to mistake obedience for peace and records for truth.

Oath-Crown

Old oath-power, binding authority, broken sanctity.

Oath-Crown turns sacred promise toward control. It is what happens when vow becomes chain.

The Cinder Host

War-force of ash, smoke, fear and wolf-skull law.

The Cinder Host teaches villages that surrender has a name. Its roads smell of burned choices and hunger given command.

The Rootbound

Crown-root horror, living captivity.

The Rootbound carry one of the saga’s oldest fears: that a living thing can be held so long it is mistaken for part of the prison.

The Underroot

Buried network, root-law, old pressure beneath the world.

The Underroot does not always attack from above. It waits below foundations, beneath names, under memory and blood.

Súsanna Rótmóðir / Root-Mother

Ancient architect of buried wickedness.

Súsanna Rótmóðir is older than Crown law and colder than ordinary hunger. She seeks unity without consent, wholeness without choice.

Root-Born, Sentinels & Deep Root

Underroot servants, watchers and foundation horror.

These are the shapes root-pressure takes when the buried law reaches upward and gives hunger a body, a guard or a foundation.

The Wrong Un’s

Moral rot wearing village faces.

The Wrong Un’s prove that evil is not always crowned. Sometimes it nods politely in daylight and waits for its phrase.

The Agreement

False mercy, ash-law, surrender made gentle.

The Agreement offers relief with hooks inside it. Its danger is that some of what it gives is real.

The Hollow

Inner command, old family wound, voice of breaking.

The Hollow does not need a throne. It lives in the old command to break, keep, obey or become useful to pain.

Grey Cradle / Thorn-Cradle Company

Institutional harm, child-law enemy.

Grey Cradle turns care into procedure and procedure into a cage. Thorn-Cradle is where soft words grow thorns.

Article Ash-Seven

Burned law, custody trap, legal ash.

Article Ash-Seven proves that a clause can be a weapon, and that missing safeguards can bleed louder than written words.

The Absence / The World Without Root

Reality judging what is worth keeping.

In the War Without Root, the world does not always kill. It asks whether a place, name or person matters enough to remain.

The House

Home, wound, memory, inner battlefield.

Some enemies are not kingdoms. Some are rooms, stairs, hinges, remembered voices and the old command to become small.

Cold Hearth Hall

Blue-grey fire, frozen mercy, clan horror.

The fires do not go out. That is the cruelty. Cold Hearth Hall burns beautifully, wrongly, and without enough warmth to save the people staring into it.

Skeld-Fell Ransom Faction

Desperation sharpened into permission.

Skeld-Fell is not simple evil. Its danger is worse: suffering used as a reason to make children pay what no child owes.

Ransom House of Snow

House of taking, winter claim, child-payment threat.

The Ransom House of Snow asks the oldest false question: whose child can be traded for someone else’s survival?

Book XIV: In Development

Book XIV has not started yet, so these entries are marked as future-road names and planned powers. They are included here as a teaser, not as finished-book material.

Jórga Rósahaddr / Pink-Head

Beast-Druid of the Salt-Marsh, old animal-law speaker.

Jórga is practical, sharp, strange and half-feral in manners. Animals trust her faster than people do, which may be the wisest thing about them.

Glimra

Mýrhlébarði, Salt-Marsh Sabre-Leopard, Veiltooth.

A massive furred sabre-cat with curved ivory killing-teeth and glimmering camouflage fur. Glimra can stand two feet away unseen, because the world around her seems to agree to lie.

Marek Vey-Corren

Hidden Raven-Crow family line.

Marek belongs to Elland’s hidden family thread, where bloodline must be witnessed but never mistaken for ownership.

Hedda Vey-Corren

Hidden family, ransom-threat witness.

Hedda stands in the coming road north, where desperation will try to dress itself as need and still be refused permission.

Kelda Vey-Corren

Water-sign child, hidden family thread.

Kelda’s name belongs to the future road of snow, family law and old agreements under mountain stone.

Finnr Vey-Corren

Hidden family, child-law stakes.

Finnr helps carry Book XIV’s central truth: no one is owed as winter payment, and rescue must never become ownership.

Krágr Halveth

Old-blood marsh father, warm face with inherited shadow.

Krágr belongs to a travelling marsh family of remedies, charms and old influence, trusted before the truth beneath them begins to stir.

Særa Veydís

Marsh mother, practical warmth, dangerous inheritance.

Særa walks the line between trusted care and older influence, where medicine and charm may carry more than anyone intended.

Lilja Crowmere

Possible fracture, redemption and resistance point.

Lilja senses wrongness in the old family trade. She may become the one who proves inherited danger does not have to become obedience.

Jórrik Salt-Marsh

Inherited root-danger, agreement instinct.

Jórrik is not simple evil, but he is tied instinctively to old agreement behaviour. Danger can be inherited before it is chosen.
