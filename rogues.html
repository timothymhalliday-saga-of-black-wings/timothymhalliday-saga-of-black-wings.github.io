Welcome to the Black Wings Saga

The Saga of Black Wings follows FATAS, Ness and the Braid through a world where power wears many masks: crowns, roots, laws, ash, houses, ledgers and smiling forms of mercy that arrive with chains hidden beneath the cloth.

Across the saga, children, beasts, spirits, warriors and wounded families stand against systems that try to count them, own them, erase them or make their pain useful.

“Care freely given. Never ransom. Never proof.”

The World

Vardrum is a realm of salt-marsh roads, old druid law, broken halls, beast-witness, hidden rivers, watchful birds and dangerous houses. Its enemies are not always monsters. Sometimes they are systems. Sometimes they are traditions. Sometimes they are the old answer everyone stopped questioning.

The Road So Far

The Saga of Black Wings is a dark epic fantasy sequence following FATAS, Ness and the Braid through wars of crown, root, ash, law, family and witness.

Books I to XIII are complete. Book XIV is in development.

Finished Books

Book I

Saga of Black Wings

The first road opens: ravens, wolves, ash, danger and the beginning of the Braid’s refusal to bow to powers that call cruelty order.

Book II

The Reckoner’s Knot

Knots of law, debt, precedent and violence tighten around the living, and refusal begins to carry a sharper price.

Book III

The Crown of Thorns and Salt

The Crown’s reach widens, old salt-roads stir and the saga’s deeper laws begin to bite.

Book IV

The Crown Unmade

Old birds, old signs, Braid-Hand, hidden histories and crown-breaking truths move into the light.

Book V

The War of the Underroot

The buried world wakes. Roots, bloodlines, old commands and ancient hunger reach upward.

Book VI

The Rising Root

The Underroot grows bolder, and the Braid learns that some wars begin beneath the floor.

Book VII

The Severing War

Severing becomes both weapon and mercy as the war against root-command grows savage.

Book VIII

The Last Root

The final root-pressure gathers, testing witness, family, refusal and every law the Braid has carried.

Book IX

The War Without Root

A new wrongness rises where the world itself seems to ask what deserves to remain.

Book X

The Quieting World

Silence, absence and hidden judgment press against the living, and the Braid refuses a world made easier by erasure.

Book XI

The Agreement of Ash

Ash offers mercy with terms beneath it. The Braid learns that peace can become another kind of prison.

Book XII

The Last Refusal

Children, names, breath, care and witness stand at the edge of final settling, and refusal becomes sacred ground.

Book XIII

The House That Raised the Raven

FATAS and Ness go home, where childhood, family wounds, inner monsters and old rooms become the battlefield.

In Development

Book XIV

The House Beyond the House

Book XIV has not started yet. The road ahead turns toward Elland’s hidden family, Skeld-Fell, cold hearths, beast-law, ransom, snow, old northern secrets and the question of what bloodline can witness without owning.

Core law: desperation is not permission.

Those Who Refuse

This hall keeps the deepest twists hidden while naming the people, animals, spirits and old companions who shape the Saga of Black Wings.

The Braid & Chosen Family

FATAS

The Raven, the Ferocious, the wounded heart of refusal.

Scarred by old houses and sharpened by survival, FATAS carries love, rage, law, tenderness and danger in the same hands.

Ness

Hearth, witness, love that does not kneel to fear.

Ness is warmth with teeth when needed, the heart beside FATAS, and one of the saga’s clearest answers to false mercy.

Eldwyn

Watcher of old law, reader of signs.

Eldwyn sees where others look away, carrying ancient knowledge, grief, wit and the burden of patterns that return.

Ulrike Ellandsdóttir Róthvaldr

Law-reader, truth-bearer, old learning sharpened.

Ulrike brings discipline, knowledge and witness to a world that often tries to make law serve harm.

Mia

One of the divided daughters, guarded heart, pattern-breaker.

Mia carries tenderness and danger under a careful surface, watched closely by animals and bound to choices that shake old systems.

Myah

Druid-born, close to Mia, bound to a deep family wound.

Myah belongs to one of the saga’s most painful love-and-family threads. Her full road is best discovered in the books.

Maisie

Divided daughter, fierce witness, no easy forgiveness.

Maisie carries judgement with a clean edge. She does not let love become excuse, and she does not let return erase harm.

James

Return-witness, quiet mystery, northern road unresolved.

James returns carrying shame, old signs, Jack’s shadow and truths that cannot be spent too quickly.

Cael

Road-companion, old knowledge, loyal pressure.

Cael stands where old travel, humour, skill and loyalty meet.

Brokan

Companion of strength, road and battle.

Brokan brings weight, loyalty and battle-memory into the Braid’s hardest roads.

Lúsaith / Lark

Druid song, hidden strength, old grief.

Lúsaith carries music, mystery and a future-thread that echoes beyond one book.

Timóði

The Forgotten Prince, guardian of Lúsaith.

Timóði stands close to Lúsaith’s road, bound by loyalty, sorrow and old royal shadow.

Taff of Winchmore / Salt-Raven

Silent-sign bearer, Braid-Hand witness.

Deaf, sharp and bound to older journeys, Taff carries signs that connect lost knowledge with present danger.

Jórunn Vánesja

Old-druid scent-bearer.

Jórunn knows what scent, beast-law and old warning can say before ordinary language catches up.

Family, Children & Witnessed Lives

Katy

Family-thread, unborn-anchor pressure.

Katy’s road touches the saga’s deepest questions of children, future, protection and inheritance.

Jenny & Josie

Family-child thread.

Jenny and Josie belong among the witnessed lives whose names matter because the saga refuses to let children become background.

Craig, Sarah, Lily & Joey

Book XII family group.

A family-thread carried through witness, danger and the refusal to let harm write the only record.

Edda, Evin, Lysa, Oris & Tovin

Children who would not become proof.

Their road insists that rescue is not theatre, care is not evidence and names belong to the named.

No Name Yet

Name decision, not absence.

“No name yet” is not emptiness. It is a boundary, a decision and a refusal to be filed too soon.

Little Hearth

Small life, enormous law.

Little Hearth carries the saga’s insistence that care is not small just because the cared-for are small.

Animal Witnesses & Beast-Kin

Mad

Old-magic dog, impossible witness.

Mad is not decoration. He knows things, refuses wrongness and makes silence answer by standing where truth breathes.

Benson

Dog-witness, loyal judge of wrongness.

Benson notices what people try to smooth over. His stillness can be louder than speech.

Hugo

Threshold dog, patient watcher.

Hugo knows that waiting can be a law, not weakness.

Jack

Moor-Wolf, northern mystery, magnificent witness.

Jack is not anyone’s ornament. He carries old signs, fierce judgement and a family-line still opening.

Blue

Ancient crow, Braid-Hand witness.

Blue is clever, rude when required and often knows more than anyone would like.

Scarlet

Ancient raven, rune-knower.

Scarlet carries old wing-memory, rune-sense and fierce loyalty.

Dusk, Mylo, Shane & Mother Hiss

Small witnesses, large truths.

The saga’s animals do not merely accompany the Braid. They witness, refuse, expose and choose.

Glimra

Salt-Marsh Sabre-Leopard, Veiltooth.

Glimra is a massive camouflaged sabre-cat whose fur shifts with reed-shadow, fog, mud, waterlight, moss, ash, snow and stone.

Spirits, Old Watchers & Returning Dead

Kian

Spirit-warrior, dire-need guide.

Kian returns where the road grows dangerous enough for old loyalty to cross the boundary.

Bella

Flame-spirit, Braid guide.

Bella carries fire, guidance and old witness where ordinary sight is not enough.

Elland

Old watcher, secret-bearer, druid shadow.

Elland’s secrets do not stay buried. His road opens wider in Book XIV.

Jorn, Sampher, Williem & The Seer

Old presences and strange returns.

The dead, the watchers and the old-voiced do not always leave the living to walk alone.

Book XIV: New Road

Jórga Rósahaddr

Pink-Head, Rose-Haired, Beast-Druid of the Salt-Marsh.

Jórga is an old-magic animal whisperer who trusts beasts faster than people. She enters Book XIV with Glimra at her heel and becomes pivotal in the final battle.

Marek, Hedda, Kelda & Finnr Vey-Corren

Elland’s hidden family-line.

The Vey-Correns draw Book XIV northward, into bloodline, ransom, winter sickness and the law that family is witness, not ownership.

Krágr, Særa, Lilja & Jórrik

Northern pressure, clan and cold-road figures.

Names from the Book XIV road, tied to Skeld-Fell, old cold and the dangerous question of what need may ask from the innocent.

The Enemy Has Many Faces

In the Saga of Black Wings, evil is not always a king with a blade. Sometimes it is a law. Sometimes it is a room. Sometimes it is a kindness with hooks hidden under the cloth.

This page keeps the deepest twists hidden, but it names the powers, tyrants, systems and old hungers that make the Braid’s road so dangerous.

“Fear the hand that calls a chain shelter.”

Crowns, Tyrants & Human Monsters

The Crown

Power dressed as order.

The Crown is more than a ruler. It is a machine of obedience, ledgers, corridors, permissions and punishments.

Oath-Crown

Vow turned into chain.

Oath-Crown is what happens when sacred promise is bent into control.

The Duchess

Polished command, cultivated cruelty.

The Duchess is refinement sharpened into domination, the kind of power that calls suffering design.

Doc

Clean coin, trained hounds, calm cruelty.

Doc is frightening because he is patient. His rooms obey too well. His dogs listen too quickly.

Hrothar Vael

The Cinder-King.

Hrothar Vael waits behind smoke roads and burned choices.

Skorn Grey-Tooth

Ash-reader, road-hunter.

Skorn belongs to the first road’s hunting pressure, where ash becomes trail and fear becomes instruction.

Torvik Brandhand

Burned hand, flame-threat.

Torvik Brandhand sounds like a man who has made pain into a signature.

Maera Salt-Eye

Watcher of salt, road and consequence.

Maera Salt-Eye belongs to the hunted coast and early road.

Eiðarr / Sawtooth

Poisoner of happiness.

Eiðarr spoils joy because joy proves people are not his.

Blómblaða of Vardrum

Petal, druid gone wrong, poisoner of family.

Blómblaða turns family-law venomous. Where Eiðarr poisons happiness, she poisons kinship.

Root-Horrors & Buried Powers

The Underroot

Buried network, command beneath the world.

The Underroot waits below foundations, below names, below memory.

Súsanna Rótmóðir / Root-Mother

Ancient architect of buried wickedness.

Súsanna Rótmóðir hungers for unity without consent.

The Rootbound

Living captivity, root-warped survival.

The Rootbound are living things held so long that the prison begins to look like part of them.

Root-Born

Servants of root-pressure.

Root-Born are hunger given limbs, proof that buried law can reach upward.

Sentinels

Watchers of the Underroot.

Sentinels are the watching instinct of a buried system.

Deep Root

Foundation horror, old pressure, buried will.

Deep Root waits where foundations think they are safe.

Ash, Agreement & False Mercy

The Agreement

False mercy, ash-law, surrender made gentle.

The Agreement is dangerous because it does offer relief. That is the hook.

Article Ash-Seven

Legal ash, custody trap, written harm.

Article Ash-Seven proves that a clause can be a weapon.

Alistair Venn

Reasonable man beside the bowl.

Alistair Venn arranges surrender until it resembles courtesy.

Morcant Veyr

Thorn-Cradle rot, old druid harm.

Morcant Veyr carries old cages, sleep-heavy milk and soft words.

Grey Cradle

Institutional care turned cage.

Grey Cradle knows how to speak softly while making children smaller.

Thorn-Cradle Company

Child-road enemy, soft name with thorns.

Thorn-Cradle is where care becomes keeping, and keeping becomes ownership.

Wrongness Without Thrones

The Wrong Un’s

Moral rot wearing village faces.

The Wrong Un’s prove evil is not always crowned.

The Unkept

Harm never answered for.

The Unkept are what grows when wrong is left unnamed long enough to feel ordinary.

The Unbound

Freedom without witness, harm without restraint.

The Unbound thrive in gaps where no one asks who paid for the peace.

The Unwitnessed

Vanishing by neglect.

The Unwitnessed are the names the world almost lets slip away.

The Unrepaid

Debt without justice, harm without answer.

The Unrepaid carry the bitter truth that not every wound receives an answer unless someone insists.

The Absence

Reality deciding what matters.

The Absence asks whether a place, person or name matters enough to remain.

Inner Monsters, Houses & Living Places

The Hollow

Inner command, old family wound.

The Hollow lives where pain learned to speak with authority.

The House

Home as wound, memory as battlefield.

Some enemies are stairs, hinges, rooms, old voices and childhood fear under the floorboards.

The Strong Thing

Protector turned dangerous if left ungoverned.

The Strong Thing wanted blood because blood once failed the child. It cannot be allowed to rule.

The Ledger

Counting as harm, record without mercy.

The Ledger is the old hunger to make people fit into numbers, marks, categories and proof.

Skeld-Fell Ransom Faction

Desperation sharpened into permission.

Skeld-Fell is dangerous because its suffering is real. The Braid must answer need without obeying ransom.

Cold Hearth Hall

Blue-grey fire, frozen mercy.

The fires do not go out. That is the cruelty.

Ransom House of Snow

House of taking, winter claim.

The Ransom House asks whose child can be traded for someone else’s survival.

The True Cold Beneath Skeld-Fell

Older bargain under stone, river and beast-line.

The Hidden Betrayal

Family plus family, love-thread danger.

Some betrayals do not enter through the gate. They sit by the fire first.
