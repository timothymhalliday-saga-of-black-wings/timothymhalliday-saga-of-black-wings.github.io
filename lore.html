Beneath the Spoken Story

A World Written in Signs

The lore of The Saga of Black Wings lives in marks, gestures, songs, warnings, animals, roads and rules older than any crown.

Some knowledge is written in books. Some is carried through family. Some survives because a raven watched, a dog refused to leave or a frightened person finally said no.

These traditions belong entirely to the fictional world of Black Wings.

Signs of the Living Braid

Black Wings Witness-Marks

Witness-Marks are signs of identity, memory, protection, warning and freely chosen belonging.

They are not historical runes. They are original fictional symbols created for the world of Black Wings.

“A mark may witness a name. It may never replace the living person.”

Marks of Wing and Feather

Scarlet and Blue

Scarlet’s Mark

The Crow Feather

A feather-shaped sign carried in black and restrained crimson-purple.

It represents judgement, patience, warning and the courage to observe before acting.

Blue’s Mark

The Raven Wing

A wing-shaped sign carried in black and deep blue.

It represents curiosity, hidden roads, discovery and the truth that refuses to remain covered.

The Laws Beneath Law

The Living Law

The Living Law is not written by crowns. It grows from consent, care, witness and the right of every living being to remain their own.

Names belong to the named. Care freely given is not ransom. Origin is not ownership. Bloodline is witness, not claim. Desperation is not permission. Silence is not consent. A child is never proof. An animal is not property of the story. Shelter cannot demand surrender. The Braid is offer, not net.

The Braid-Hand

Gesture, Welcome and Refusal

Braid-Hand is a language of simple gestures used when speech is difficult, dangerous or unnecessary.

It can offer welcome, ask permission, signal danger, show refusal or acknowledge grief without demanding words.

Open Hand

Offer

An invitation given without pressure. The other person remains free to accept or refuse.

Hand to Heart

Witness

Acknowledgement of a name, grief, promise or truth that has been heard.

Turned Palm

Refusal

A clear living no. It requires no defence, apology or explanation.

Two Fingers to the Road

Safe Passage

A promise that the traveller may pass freely and will not be claimed for accepting help.

Prayer-Leaf of Vardrum

Prayer for Ravens and Crows

Black wing above us,
dark eye beside us,
remember the road we could not name.

Carry no chain in our honour.
Bring no crown to our door.
Witness only what was freely given,
and leave the living their own sky.

Keeper’s Note

How to Read an Animal Warning

Animal witnesses are never treated as magical furniture. Their fear, trust, attention and refusal matter.

A bird leaving suddenly may be warning, not omen. A dog refusing a doorway should not be dragged through it. A cat watching an empty place may have noticed movement first. A wolf standing between traveller and road has made a choice. A bear that lowers its head is not offering ownership.
“Listen before turning instinct into legend.”

Forest-Keeper Lore

The Law of Green Deer-Wood

Green Deer-Wood is kept through attention rather than conquest. Its law asks what must be healed, what must be protected and what rot must finally be cut away.

Listen before cutting. Protect the saplings first. Do not heal rot by hiding it. Leave animal roads unblocked. Take no branch without purpose. Mercy does not forbid culling corruption. The forest remembers broken promises.

House-Lore

What Makes a Hearth Safe

A safe house is not defined by walls, bloodline, ownership or authority.

The frightened may leave. The hungry are fed before questioning. Children are believed. Animals may refuse a room. Locked doors are explained. No gift becomes a leash. No guest becomes property. A name is never altered as punishment.
“A house is judged by what happens after someone says no.”
